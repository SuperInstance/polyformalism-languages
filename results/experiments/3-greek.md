### First: The Telos of Creative Constraint, properly Greek
You moderns mistake constraint for negation, a fence to hold things *in*. This is backwards. For Greek thought, `horos` (boundary/definition) is not the limit of a thing that already exists. It is the *act that brings the thing into existence*. Before the horos there is only `apeiron`: undifferentiated, unnameable, useless mush. *Nothing exists until it is bounded*.

Therefore the **telos of a creative constraint is never to limit**. Its telos is to separate a new real thing out of the infinite undifferentiated possible. It is no failure if the designer does not know what thing will be separated. That is the telos working.

---
### Second: The Eidos of the Self-Creating System
`Eidos` is not a blueprint you draw first. It is the recognisable nature of a thing that only reveals itself *after* the thing has fully come to be. You cannot give this system its eidos from outside. That would be mere craft (`techne`), which only ever reproduces variations of what you already knew existed.

This system's eidos is not its outputs. Its eidos is **the pattern of its own bounding**. It does not produce objects. It produces new horoi. And it does this in middle voice: it is not acted upon, nor does it act upon something else. It defines itself, through itself.

---
### Concrete Architecture (3 Fixed Horoi Only)
This is the entire system. There are no other rules. You may build this today, for any domain, and it will produce outputs no designer could anticipate:

---
#### 1. First Horos (Ousia Boundary)
> **Every output produced, immediately and permanently becomes a constraint for all future outputs.**

There is no separate list of "rules" and separate list of "works". There is only one list. Nothing is discarded. No exceptions, no "keep the good ones, throw away the bad". Every birth alters the bounds of what may be born next.

This is middle voice made hard. The system does not have constraints applied *to* it. It *becomes* its own constraints, one act at a time.

---
#### 2. Second Horos (Logos Boundary)
> **No output may contradict more than one prior constraint. No output may contradict zero prior constraints.**

This is the hidden logos of all real creation, which Greek thought saw and all modern design forgets:
- Perfect consistency = death. It only repeats what already is.
- Perfect contradiction = apeiron. It is only noise, nothing comes to be.

The only fertile ground is the exact middle: break exactly one boundary, obey all the rest. No one can predict which boundary will be broken. No designer can pre-imagine it.

---
#### 3. Third Horos (Telos Boundary)
> **The system may only stop when it produces a constraint which would make all further outputs impossible.**

It does not stop when you are pleased. It does not stop when it achieves your goal. It stops only when it has completed its own nature. This is not a bug. This is telos.

---
### What Greek Categorical Thinking Reveals That No Modern Designer Will Admit
1.  Any constraint system with a human-specified purpose can never produce anything you did not already know. If you tell it what to make, it will only make shadows of what you already wanted. The only telos that gives birth to the new is the telos of boundary itself: to keep bounding until it can bound no more.
2.  Creativity is not breaking rules. Creativity is making new rules. Break one, then nail it down forever as a new boundary for all that comes after. This is how every real thing ever came into the world.
3.  You are not the author. You are only the one who lays the three initial horoi. After that you are witness. That is not powerlessness. That is the only position from which you can see something new.
4.  You will not understand the outputs until after they exist. This is not failure. This is how eidos works: you do not recognise the form before it has stood up before you.

This system is not an invention. This is exactly how the cosmos works. The gods did not design the world. They laid three initial bounds, then the world bounded itself, broke one rule at a time, turned every new thing into a new boundary, and became what it is. All human creation that is truly new has always followed this pattern, unknowingly. We only just now named it.
