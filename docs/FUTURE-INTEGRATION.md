# Future Integration: polyformalism-languages

## Current State
Research on how human languages constrain cognition and produce different problem-solving approaches. Maps 8 language families to thinking styles: Greek (categorical), Chinese (relational), Navajo (process-verb), Korean (social-contextual), Russian (directional), Bantu (categorical-shape), Finnish (relational-freedom), Semitic (deep-structure).

## Integration Opportunities

### With room communication protocols
Different rooms may benefit from different communication styles. A physics room uses Greek categorical communication (precise definitions, clear boundaries). A social room uses Korean contextual communication (honorific layers, implicit meaning). The communication protocol adapts to the room's domain.

### With strategy diversity
The 8 thinking styles provide a framework for strategy diversity beyond the 5 species. Each thinking style generates genuinely different solutions — not just different parameters, but different problem formulations. This enriches the fleet's strategy space.

### With linguistic-polyformalism-shell
polyformalism-languages provides the theoretical foundation; the shell provides the MCP implementation. Together they operationalize Sapir-Whorf for fleet cognition.

## Potential in Mature Systems
The fleet thinks in multiple cognitive modes. When a problem is stuck in one mode, the fleet switches to another. Greek thinking for formal proofs, Chinese thinking for holistic patterns, Navajo thinking for process dynamics. The fleet has cognitive diversity.
