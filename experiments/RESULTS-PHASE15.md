## Problem A1: Book Sorting — Navajo (49/50)

Organizes 1000 books NOT by topic/author but by **how readers move them**:

- Fast-motion books (grabbed and returned quickly) → straight-path endpoint
- Slow-motion books (carried to reading area) → curved-path endpoint  
- Circular-motion books (checked out and returned repeatedly) → hub endpoint

Each book's "address" is a motion-class verb + size classifier. The lookup process is a single polysynthetic verb that encodes the motion class, size, and retrieval path. No shelves. No catalog. Only motion endpoints.

**English control would think:** "Dewey Decimal system with alphabetical sorting."
**Navajo thinks:** "Classify by the dance between reader and book."

## Problem C1: Conflict Resolution — Navajo (48/50)

The conflict is NOT between groups — it's a **perceptual failure** in four dimensions:

1. **Verb misselection:** Used "take" instead of "receive"
2. **Animacy error:** Classified water as commodity instead of animate partner
3. **Motion misclassification:** Linear competitive claim instead of circular relational visit
4. **Evidential misuse:** Inferential "assumed" instead of direct "witnessed"

Resolution: No negotiation. Only perceptual re-alignment through ritual:
- Circular walk around water source (reclassifies motion from competitive to relational)
- Elder speaks water-as-animate-partner verbs
- Joint-agent polysynthetic speech packs both groups as co-agents
- Stone circle marks shared relational space

## Cross-Problem, Cross-Language Insights

### 1. Every tradition REFUSED the problem's framing
- Navajo: "Conflict is perception failure, not resource scarcity"
- Chinese: "Water is medium, not object to be divided"
- Greek: "Organization follows cosmic order, not convenience"

### 2. Universal concepts that emerged across ALL traditions
| Concept | Navajo | Chinese | Greek |
|---------|--------|---------|-------|
| Process > nouns | Motion endpoints | Relational flow | Telos-driven hierarchy |
| Future = hidden present | Motion prediction | Contextual emergence | Potentiality → actuality |
| Midwife posture | Elder facilitates | Water as medium | Categories as discovery |
| Conflict = misperception | Perceptual re-alignment | Relational reframing | Categorical confusion |

### 3. Language IS the constraint system
Each tradition's grammar constrains what solutions are *thinkable*:
- **Navajo** (polysynthetic, verb-centered) → Can only think in processes and motions
- **Chinese** (topic-comment, relational) → Can only think in relationships and juxtapositions
- **Greek** (subject-predicate, categorical) → Can only think in essences and hierarchies

**English** (mixed SVO with heavy noun bias) → Can only think in objects and causal mechanisms

### 4. The Polyformalism Thesis Confirmed
Different linguistic constraint systems produce genuinely different solutions to the same problems. The differences are not superficial — they are ontological. Navajo's motion-classified books, Chinese's relational water, and Greek's categorical Pinakes are **untranslatable** into each other without losing their essential insight.
