# Reverse Actualization Experiment: Book Sorting (A1)

**Problem:** Organize 1000 books so anyone can find any book within 2 minutes.
**Date:** 2026-05-07
**Model:** Seed-2.0-mini (DeepInfra)

---

## Ancient Greek Thinking — Score: 48/50

**Core framing:** Categorical hierarchy based on Aristotle's branches of knowledge. Binary oppositions (theoretical/practical, general/particular, natural/human). Telos = universal access for eudaimonia.

**Key insight:** The solution is literally the Library of Alexandria's *Pinakes* catalog system (Callimachus, 3rd century BCE). 10 major categories → 100 subcategories → 1000 books, using Pythagorean sacred number proportionality (10→100→1000). Each category maps to Aristotle's branches: theology, natural philosophy, mathematics, ethics, economics, politics, rhetoric, poetics, history, craft.

**The 2-minute search:** Identify category (10s) → walk to bay (20s) → scan 10 shelf labels (20s) → walk to shelf (10s) → scan 10 books (30s) → retrieve (10s) = 100 seconds, 20s spare.

**Would be impossible in Navajo because:** Navajo has no noun-centered categories — it would organize by the *process of reading* or *motion of pages* rather than *essence of content*.
